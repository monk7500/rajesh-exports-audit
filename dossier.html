I. Executive Summary

Rajesh Exports Limited (REL) is a Bengaluru-headquartered gold jewellery manufacturer and exporter, founded in 1995 and listed on NSE and BSE. Through its 2015 acquisition of Valcambi SA — the world's largest gold refinery, headquartered in Switzerland — REL projected itself as one of India's largest companies by consolidated revenue, routinely reporting ₹2–3 lakh crore turnover.

On 3 June 2026, SEBI issued a 109-page ex parte interim order (WTM/KCV/IVD-1/001/2026-27) alleging that REL misrepresented approximately ₹15.15 lakh crore (~USD 180 billion) in subsidiary-linked revenues between FY2021 and FY2025 — equivalent to roughly 99.80% of all revenues attributed to overseas subsidiaries. The order bars both REL and its Executive Chairman Rajesh Mehta from the securities markets.

This dossier consolidates all known allegations against REL across regulatory agencies, courts, and investigative media from 2013 to 2026. It covers 14 distinct allegations spanning revenue inflation, fund diversion, fake accounting entries, DRI customs violations, Karnataka tax evasion raids, and PLI scheme eligibility.

II. Active Allegations (SEBI — June 2026)

A-001
Revenue Inflation via Valcambi SA / Overseas Subsidiaries
Active
Authority
SEBI — Interim Order dated 3 June 2026 (WTM/KCV/IVD-1/001/2026-27)
Period
FY 2020-21 to FY 2024-25 (5 years)
Quantum
₹15,15,385 crore (~USD 180 billion) — alleged misrepresentation of subsidiary revenue
Allegation
Between 97–99% of REL's consolidated revenues were attributed to overseas subsidiaries, primarily Valcambi SA (Switzerland) and Global Gold Refineries AG (GGR). SEBI found Valcambi's own audited standalone financials showed only a small fraction — e.g. CY2023 standalone revenue ~₹542.68 crore vs GGR consolidated ~₹2.93 lakh crore.
Structure
REL India → REL Singapore PTE Ltd (95%) → Global Gold Refineries AG (Switzerland) → Valcambi SA. REL Singapore and intermediate entities found to have little or no substantive operations.
Co. Response
Valcambi recognises only processing/value-addition revenue; GGR records gross gold transaction value. SEBI found this prima facie unsupported by records or accounting logic.
Law
SEBI Act, 1992 ss. 11, 11B; LODR Regs. 4(1)(c), 30, 33; PFUTP Regulations, 2003
Impact
₹12,726 crore estimated shareholder wealth erosion; stock down over 50% year-on-year
A-002
Fake Accounting Entries — Affluence Shares and Stocks Pvt. Ltd.
Active
Authority
SEBI — Interim Order, 3 June 2026
Period
FY 2021-22 to FY 2023-24
Quantum
Sales: ₹11,486.60 crore; Purchases: ₹11,488.42 crore (~66–67% of standalone revenues)
Allegation
REL's standalone accounts recorded ₹11,487–11,488 crore transactions with Affluence Shares & Stocks Pvt. Ltd. Affluence denied ever having a business relationship with REL. Sales and purchases were nearly equal — no real value addition — the textbook signature of fictitious entries.
SEBI Analysis
Entries linked to promoter Rajesh Mehta's personal gold derivative trades, recorded in REL's books as company transactions to artificially inflate standalone turnover.
Co. Explanation
Trades intended for MCX but routed through Mehta's personal Affluence account due to MCX litigation. REL transferred ₹7.5 crore to Mehta's personal account for this purpose.
Law
SEBI PFUTP Regulations, 2003; Companies Act, 2013 ss. 128, 129, 134; LODR Regulations
A-003 / A-004
Fund Diversion to Promoter Personal Accounts — Rajesh Mehta & Siddharth Mehta
Active
Authority
SEBI — Interim Order, 3 June 2026
Quantum
₹339 crore routed via Rajesh Mehta's personal accounts; total undisclosed related transactions ₹926 crore
Allegation
Company funds transferred from REL's corporate accounts to personal bank accounts of Rajesh Mehta and Siddharth Mehta without board approval, audit committee approval, or investor disclosure. Funds used for Mehta's personal derivative trading.
Gov. Breach
REL's own MD Suresh Gowda and CFO Vijendra Rao stated in depositions they were unaware of material transactions. REL admitted certain transfers were made "without revealing the bank account from which the funds had come."
Law
Companies Act, 2013 ss. 177, 188; LODR Reg. 23 (related-party transactions); SEBI Act s. 11B
A-005
Opaque Related-Party Transactions — Elest Pvt. Ltd.
Active
Authority
SEBI — Interim Order, 3 June 2026
Period
FY21 to FY26
Quantum
REL transferred ₹565.88 crore to Elest; Elest returned ₹350.03 crore → net outflow ₹215.85 crore
Background
Elest Pvt. Ltd. incorporated October 2020 by Rajesh Mehta and Prashant Mehta. Engaged in lithium-ion cell manufacturing, battery packs, and EVs. SEBI classified as a related party of REL.
SEBI Finding
Fund movements not adequately disclosed as related-party transactions. Board and audit committee approvals not obtained. Responses filed 17 March 2026 require further verification.
Law
LODR Reg. 23; Companies Act, 2013 s. 188; SEBI Circular on Related Party Transactions
A-006
Cross-Holding Scheme — ACC Energy Storage Pvt. Ltd.
Active
Authority
SEBI — Interim Order, 3 June 2026
Period
1 January 2025
Quantum
Elest transferred ₹147 crore to ACC Energy; ₹112 crore returned same day; ACC subsequently invested ₹262 crore in Elest
Allegation
Cross-holding transferred ~48.95% effective ownership in ACC Energy to Elest (promoter-controlled private entity) without board approval or shareholder disclosure. REL's MD and CFO testified they were unaware of these transactions.
PLI Link
ACC Energy Storage Pvt. Ltd. is the vehicle holding REL's PLI entitlement under the ACC Battery Storage scheme. SEBI findings triggered MHI review (see A-010).
A-007
Unverified Gold Mine Investments in Africa
Active
Authority
SEBI / NSE — Interim Order, 3 June 2026
Quantum
₹1,035.27 crore (FY23) under 'other non-current investments' — escalated to ₹10,547.72 crore by FY25
Allegation
REL told NSE the amount represented "investment in gold mines in Africa." SEBI could not verify this from financial statements or any other available records. No valuation reports, entity-wise breakups, or asset reconciliations produced.
A-008
Failure to Disclose Subsidiary Financials (LODR / Regulation 30)
Active
Authority
SEBI — Interim Order, 3 June 2026; NSE / BSE queries
Period
FY21–FY25 (ongoing)
Allegation
Despite 97–99% of consolidated revenues flowing through overseas subsidiaries, REL did not upload financial statements of any subsidiary on its website. Repeatedly failed to furnish underlying transaction data, customer/vendor details despite multiple SEBI summons.
SEBI Note
"A listed entity cannot rely on foreign data-protection regimes (e.g., Swiss banking secrecy) to defeat statutory disclosure obligations under Indian law."
Law
LODR Regs. 2015 Reg. 30, Schedule III; Companies Act, 2013 s. 136; SEBI Circular CIR/CFD/CMD/56/2016

III. Remediated

A-009
Delayed Shareholding Pattern Filings — June & September 2025 Quarters
Remediated
Authority
NSE / BSE — Exchange communications
Allegation
REL failed to submit quarterly shareholding pattern disclosures for two consecutive quarters (June 2025 and September 2025) in breach of SEBI LODR Reg. 31. NSE issued formal communication. REL subsequently filed outstanding disclosures.
Note
The pattern of disclosure failures — even when individually remediated — strengthens SEBI's broader case regarding systemic governance deficiencies.

IV. Under Government Review

A-010
PLI Scheme Eligibility — ACC Battery Storage (MHI Review)
Under Review
Authority
Ministry of Heavy Industries (MHI)
Background
REL was a beneficiary under the PLI scheme for Advanced Chemistry Cell (ACC) battery storage — part of a ₹18,100 crore Union Government programme, executed through ACC Energy Storage Pvt. Ltd.
Trigger
SEBI's June 2026 interim order alleging fund diversion, opaque cross-holdings, and governance failures directly connected to ACC Energy and Elest triggered MHI review. Internal ministry view reportedly favours removal.
Risk
PLI removal would eliminate government-backed incentive revenues. Final decision pending Union Minister's review of SEBI findings.

V. Historical Allegations (2013–2019)

Illegal Diversion of SEZ Gold to Domestic Market — Cochin SEZ
Historical
Authority
Directorate of Revenue Intelligence (DRI), Cochin
Period
~2010–2013 (discovered May 2013)

Gold imported duty-free for export at Cochin Export Processing Zone illegally diverted to Indian domestic retail market. Senior executive caught at Cochin Railway Station carrying 900g of gold destined for Bengaluru retail.

October 2013

A-013
Tax Evasion — Karnataka Commercial Taxes Raid (D.K. Ravi)
Historical
Authority
Karnataka Commercial Taxes (Enforcement) Department
Period
February 2015
Allegation
IAS officer D.K. Ravi raided REL on 19 February 2015 for suspected tax evasion — part of a broader crackdown recovering ₹138 crore in 2 weeks across Embassy, RMZ, Prestige, Nitesh Builders, and others.

A-014
Anomalous Surge in Kochi SEZ Jewellery Exports — FY17–FY19
Historical
Authority
Financial Chronicle investigative report; flagged in SEBI context 2026
Period
FY 2016-17 to FY 2018-19
Allegation
Kochi SEZ gems & jewellery exports surged from negligible (FY16) to ₹40,888 crore (FY19) after REL commenced operations — while aggregate gold jewellery exports across all other Indian SEZs declined 6% after UAE introduced 5% VAT on gold in 2018.
Significance
The single-entity anomalous surge against a sector-wide decline in a post-VAT environment that made gold export uneconomical for other players is now consistent with SEBI's FY21–FY25 revenue misrepresentation findings.

IX. Audit Observations

7.1 — Pattern of Systemic Non-Disclosure
  • REL failed to upload subsidiary financial statements for years despite 97–99% of revenues flowing through those subsidiaries — a structural concealment of operational reality.
  • Delayed shareholding pattern filings for two consecutive quarters, combined with Elest transactions, suggest coordinated avoidance of disclosure windows.
  • REL claimed 'gold mines in Africa' for ₹1,035 crore without a single supporting document. This escalated to ₹10,547 crore by FY25.
7.2 — The Valcambi Revenue Architecture
  • Valcambi processes ~2,000 tonnes/year. At prevailing gold prices this represents USD 55–60 billion in gross throughput. However, Valcambi's own audited accounts show only a fraction as revenue — it recognises processing fees, not gross gold value.
  • Whether REL's consolidation of gross gold value constitutes genuine revenue depends on Ind AS 115 revenue recognition criteria — a critical factual dispute for the forensic audit to resolve.
  • SEBI's finding is prima facie — it means the methodology and documentation were not produced in response to summons, not that fraud is conclusively established.
7.3 — The Affluence Entries: No Legitimate Explanation
  • Unlike the Valcambi dispute (which has an accounting methodology defence), the Affluence entries have none. Affluence explicitly denied any relationship. Sales and purchases nearly equal — no value addition — the textbook signature of fictitious entries.
  • These entries constituted 66–67% of REL's standalone revenues in the relevant period, making the standalone financial statements substantially unreliable.
7.4 — The Elest–ACC Scheme: Promoter Enrichment
  • The cross-holding, executed on a single date (1 January 2025), transferred effective majority control of ACC Energy — a public-company subsidiary with PLI benefits — to a privately held promoter entity, without board approval or shareholder disclosure.
  • The same-day ₹147 crore → ₹112 crore round-trip bears hallmarks of artificial capital inflation to justify the ₹60/share valuation for parallel equity subscriptions.
7.5 — Historical Pattern: Not an Isolated Event
  • The 2013 DRI case, the 2015 Karnataka tax raid, and the FY17–FY19 Kochi SEZ surge collectively indicate that REL's governance deficiencies predate the SEBI order by over a decade.
  • The death of D.K. Ravi shortly after raiding REL, followed by reassessment of his findings, is a contextual fact that strengthens the case for independent institutional oversight of historical enforcement outcomes.
