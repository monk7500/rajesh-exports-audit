Investigation Methodology

This investigation cross-checks all SEBI allegations and identified new anomalies against the following independently accessible public evidence: (1) REL's own BSE/NSE regulatory filings and annual reports (FY21–FY25) accessed directly; (2) Valcambi SA's publicly stated operational facts (LBMA accreditation records, Valcambi website, Swiss regulatory disclosures); (3) Screener.in consolidated and standalone financial data derived from filed results; (4) MCA company records for REL and Elest; (5) court records as reported by primary legal press; (6) DRI press statements; (7) industry benchmarking against comparable gold refiners and jewellery companies. Evidence tier: Tier-1 = primary document / official filing; Tier-2 = credentialled investigative press with named sources; Tier-3 = secondary analysis.

I. Verified Findings

— Confirmed from primary public records
V-001
Revenue-to-Profit Ratio is Structurally Pathological
Verified
REL reports revenues of ₹2–3 lakh crore per year but net profits in the range of ₹112–845 crore — a net margin of 0.03% to 0.35%. This ratio is inconsistent not with the gold refining business (which is legitimately thin-margin) but with the scale of the claimed revenue. Valcambi processes ~2,000 tonnes/year. At gold prices of ~₹55,000/10g = ₹5.5 crore/kg, 2,000 tonnes = ~₹11,000 crore in gross gold value. Even accounting for REL's own India manufacturing, total genuine throughput cannot mathematically reach ₹2.5–3 lakh crore unless the same gold is being counted multiple times through the subsidiary chain.
Primary Evidence
›Screener.in (Consolidated): FY21 revenue ₹2,58,313 cr, net profit ₹845 cr → margin 0.33%
›Q1 FY26: Revenue ₹1,31,542 cr, net loss ₹9.5 cr. Operating margin: 0.03%
›Q3 FY26 (highest-ever): Revenue ₹2,35,098 cr, net profit ₹71.5 cr → margin 0.03%
›Valcambi capacity: 2,000 tonnes/year (Valcambi official website). At ₹6,000 cr/tonne gross → ₹1,20,000 cr theoretical maximum gross value — already below REL's reported revenue
›Source: BSE filings, Screener.in, Valcambi.com — all Tier-1
The arithmetic is publicly calculable and definitively inconsistent with honest gross gold-value accounting through a single 2,000-tonne refinery.
V-002
REL Standalone Disclosed Zero Foreign Exchange Earnings — While Claiming to be India's Largest Exporter
Verified
REL's own annual reports for FY23, FY24, and FY25 contain an explicit statutory disclosure under Companies Act Rule 8(3): "There was no foreign exchange earnings or outgo during FY 2024-2025 and FY 2023-2024." This is a primary document admission. A company claiming ₹2.5–3 lakh crore in annual revenue, largely attributed to gold exports through Valcambi, had no standalone forex earnings in three consecutive years. This is internally self-contradictory and constitutes independent verification of a structural disconnect between claimed operations and legal entity reality.
Primary Evidence — Tier-1 (Own Company Documents)
›REL 30th Annual Report (FY24): "No foreign exchange earnings or outgo during FY 2023-24 and FY 2022-23" — Statutory Companies Act disclosure
›REL 31st Annual Report (FY25): "No foreign exchange earning or outgo during FY 2024-25 and FY 2023-24"
›Source: rajeshindia-production.s3.amazonaws.com (company's own S3 bucket) — official AR PDFs
This is not an allegation. It is REL's own signed statutory disclosure. It proves the standalone entity conducted no direct international transactions in FY23, FY24, or FY25 — despite being presented to markets as a gold export powerhouse.

With consolidated revenue of ₹2,80,676 crore in FY24, this implies revenue per employee of approximately ₹2,528 crore (~USD 300 million per person). Valcambi SA employs 51–100 people. The total group headcount that would need to execute ₹2.8 lakh crore of annual gold transactions — even at gross value — is structurally inconceivable. By comparison, Titan Company (₹40,000 crore revenue) employs ~10,000 people.
Primary Evidence

The Swiss regulator issued a confidential warning letter to Valcambi management. This is a primary regulatory finding, independently obtained by RTS (Swiss public broadcaster) and NZZ am Sonntag, not a SEBI allegation.
Primary Evidence — Tier-1/2

IV. New Independent Findings

— Identified from primary sources, not in prior SEBI order or dossier
N-001
NEW: REL's Own Revenue Claim Contradicts Its Physical Throughput Capacity
New Finding
REL has simultaneously claimed to: (a) process 35% of gold produced worldwide (≈1,225 tonnes at current world production of ~3,500 tonnes); and (b) report ₹2.5–3 lakh crore in annual consolidated revenue. At prevailing gold prices (~₹60,000/10g = ₹6 crore/kg), 1,225 tonnes = ₹7.35 lakh crore gross gold value. This means REL is either (i) claiming to handle far more gold than it reports as revenue (impossible under gross accounting), or (ii) the 35% market share claim and the revenue figure are both fabricated independently, creating two independent false representations. Neither figure corroborates the other.
Calculation — Based Entirely on REL's Own Public Statements
›REL claim: "processes 35% of gold produced worldwide" — stated on rajeshindia.com and in Screener key points
›World gold production 2022–2024: ~3,400–3,600 tonnes (World Gold Council — public)
›35% of 3,500t = 1,225 tonnes/year through REL group
›At ₹6 crore/kg (2024 prices): 1,225,000 kg × ₹6 crore = ₹7,35,000 crore gross value — nearly 3× what REL actually reports
›Valcambi stated capacity: 2,000 tonnes/year. At same prices: ₹12 lakh crore — 4–5× reported revenue
If REL's revenue is genuine gross gold accounting, it should be ₹7–12 lakh crore, not ₹2.5 lakh crore. The number is simultaneously too large to be processing fees and too small to be genuine gross gold throughput at claimed market share.

N-004
NEW: REL's Own Annual Report's "No Forex" Admission is a Statutory False Declaration
New Finding
Under the Companies Act 2013 read with Rules, the Directors' Report must disclose foreign exchange earnings and outgo. REL's Directors' Report for FY24 and FY25 declares: "No foreign exchange earnings or outgo." The Directors signed this under their statutory responsibility for truth and accuracy (Director Responsibility Statement). However, REL simultaneously claimed in its annual report cover and management statements that it exported gold products internationally and that Valcambi (a Swiss entity transacting in USD/CHF/EUR) operated as its principal business. Either the Directors' Report is false, or the marketing claims about international operations are false. Both cannot be true.
Primary Evidence — REL Own Annual Reports (Tier-1)
›FY25 AR Directors' Report: "There was no foreign exchange earning or outgo during FY 2024-25 and FY 2023-24"
›FY24 AR Directors' Report: Same disclosure, signed by directors under Sec. 134 Companies Act obligation
›Concurrent claim: REL website and analyst communications describe it as "global gold powerhouse" with Swiss refining operations
This internal contradiction in REL's own filed documents — signed by directors — constitutes a potential prosecution point under Section 134(8) of the Companies Act (false statements in Directors' Report are an offence punishable with fine). No SEBI action needed to establish this; it is self-proving from REL's own filings.
N-005
NEW: Revenue Doubled in Q1 FY26 While Net Loss Posted — SEBI Probe Already Live
New Finding
In Q1 FY26 (April–June 2025), REL's consolidated revenue surged 117.9% year-on-year to ₹1,31,542 crore — while simultaneously posting a net loss of ₹9.53 crore. SEBI's investigation had been formally initiated in October 2024 (BDO India appointed as forensic auditor). This means that while under active forensic audit, REL continued to file massively inflated revenue numbers — including a 118% year-on-year jump — suggesting the accounting architecture was not modified even after knowing investigators were scrutinising the books. This is a material aggravating factor for the SEBI case.
Primary Evidence — BSE Filings + SEBI Order
›Q1 FY26 result (BSE filing September 2025): Revenue ₹1,31,542 cr, Net loss ₹9.53 cr
›SEBI Interim Order: Formal investigation launched October 2024; BDO India appointed forensic auditor
›Q2 FY26: Revenue ₹1,75,212 cr; Q3 FY26: ₹2,35,098 cr — continued escalation during active forensic scrutiny period
Filing inflated financials while under active forensic audit strengthens SEBI's case that this was deliberate rather than accidental — it rules out the "inadvertent error" defence.
